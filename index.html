// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your site's image carousel renders 21 slides with zero structured alt-text, no JSON-LD schema markup for `ArchitecturalFirm` or `LocalBusiness`, and no embedded EXIF location or project-type data—every image is an invisible file to Google's crawler. Right now, you are bleeding the query "sustainable architect Seattle" (or whatever your primary metro is) to competitors whose project images carry alt-text like `"LEED-certified multifamily residential architect Portland"` while your Postmark Center for the Arts and Winthrop Library shots sit as unnamed decorative assets. That's civic and institutional project work—high-contract-value categories—being indexed as nothing.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: The developer, civic planner, or library board director evaluating your firm for a public or institutional commission needs documented proof of process, not a slideshow. You claim "craftsmanship, sustainability, and human connection" but supply zero construction-phase documentation, no materials callouts, and no occupancy or completion data tied to named projects like Gladstone Library—which is exactly the kind of completed public-use project that should carry verifiable before/after visual sequencing. Without it, a procurement committee has no visual evidence to put in front of a board, and they move to the firm that gave them a PDF-ready case study.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Any regional firm pairing project photography with structured case study pages—tagged by building typology, square footage, and sustainability certification—is currently capturing the institutional RFP pipeline you're positioned to own with projects like Winthrop Library and Postmark Center for the Arts. The gap isn't aesthetic; it's that competitors are converting their photography into indexed, linkable project assets that show up in government and nonprofit procurement research cycles, while your Gear Loft Shared Roof sits in a carousel with no context, no searchable data, and no mechanism to generate an inbound inquiry.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured shoot protocol tied directly to your named project portfolio—Laurelhurst, Gladstone Library, Postmark—capturing exterior context, interior occupancy, material detail, and site relationship in a defined sequence for each typology. Every delivered image file gets embedded with standardized EXIF data (project name, location, building type, completion year) and deployed with handwritten alt-text and JSON-LD `ImageObject` markup tied to individual project schema pages. This converts your existing completed work into a crawlable, trust-building, inquiry-generating asset library rather than a silent carousel that tells Google—and your next institutional client—nothing.
